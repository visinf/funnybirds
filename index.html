FunnyBirds

A Synthetic Vision Dataset for a Part-Based Analysis of Explainable AI Methods

ICCV 2023 oral presentation

Robin Hesse1
Simone Schaub-Meyer1,2
Stefan Roth1,2
1Department of Computer Science, TU Darmstadt
2hessian.AI
GitHubPaperVideo

Addressing the challenge of evaluating XAI methods:

We create an open-source synthetic vision dataset of bird species and automatic evaluation protocols.

We enable analyzing explanations on a part level, which is closer to human comprehension than existing methods that evaluate on a pixel level.

By comparing the model output for inputs with removed parts, we can estimate ground-truth part importances that should be reflected in the explanations.

By mapping individual explanations into a common space of part importances, we can analyze a variety of different explanation types in a single common framework.

Our results

We measure explainability along five dimensions:

Accuracy (Acc.): The standard classification accuracy of the model.

Background Independence (B.I.): The ratio of background objects that are unimportant.

Completeness (Com.): Are all aspects of model's decision represented?

Correctness (Corr.): Is the explanation faithful with respect to the model?

Contrastivity (Con.): Does the explanation actually depend on the input? Are all aspects of model's decision represented?

A final score (mean explainability mX from the paper) is created as the average over Completeness, Correctness, and Contrastivity. The results are shown in the following table.
